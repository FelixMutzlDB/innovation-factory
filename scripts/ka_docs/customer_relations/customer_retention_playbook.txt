CUSTOMER RETENTION PLAYBOOK - FLEET ACCOUNTS
ASM Cockpit Customer Relations
Document Version: 1.5 | Last Updated: January 2025

OVERVIEW
Fleet customer retention is more cost-effective than acquisition. Contract renewals require proactive management. This playbook outlines strategies for retaining fleet customers at renewal and reducing churn risk throughout the contract term.

RENEWAL TIMELINE

12 Months Before Renewal
- Account review: Volume trend, satisfaction, issues
- Risk assessment: Red, yellow, green
- Competitive intelligence: Are they shopping?
- Relationship: Ensure regular contact; no surprises

6 Months Before Renewal
- Formal review: Quarterly business review format
- Value demonstration: Volume, savings, service metrics
- Early discussion: Gauge satisfaction; any concerns?
- Proposal: Begin internal pricing discussion

3 Months Before Renewal
- Proposal: Present renewal terms
- Negotiation: Per b2b_pricing_framework.txt
- Address concerns: Any issues from contract period
- Decision makers: Ensure we are talking to right people

1 Month Before Renewal
- Final terms: Confirm in writing
- Contract: Legal review, signature
- Transition: If any changes, plan implementation
- Celebration: Renewal confirmed - thank customer

RETENTION STRATEGIES

1. Proactive Issue Resolution
- Don't wait for customer to complain
- Monitor: Invoicing disputes, quality issues, station problems
- Resolve quickly: Within 24-48 hours for fleet
- Reference: customer_complaint_transeuropa_jan2025.txt - quick resolution maintained relationship

2. Value Beyond Price
- Reporting: Useful, timely, accurate
- Account management: Responsive, knowledgeable
- Station network: Right locations for their routes
- Service: 24/7 support where contract requires
- Loyalty: Ensure points credited; fleet values this

3. Relationship Depth
- Multiple contacts: Don't rely on single relationship
- Executive engagement: For key accounts, periodic senior contact
- Personal touch: Know their business, their challenges
- Reference: fleet_contract_danubia.txt - executive relationship

4. Competitive Defense
- Know the competition: What are they offering?
- Our advantages: Document and communicate
- Price: Competitive but not always lowest
- Walk-away: Know our minimum acceptable terms

5. Contract Structure
- Term: Longer term for better pricing - locks in commitment
- Volume flexibility: Some flexibility for economic cycles
- Auto-renewal: Consider for low-risk accounts
- Notice period: Adequate time to address before exit

RISK INDICATORS
- Volume decline: Below commitment for 2+ months
- Payment delay: Pattern of late payment
- Complaint escalation: Multiple or unresolved
- Contact reduction: Harder to reach, fewer meetings
- Competitive mention: "We're evaluating options"

INTERVENTION
- Red account: Escalate to regional B2B director
- Action plan: Specific steps, owner, timeline
- Executive involvement: For strategic accounts
- Concession: What can we offer to retain? (Discount, terms, service)

SUCCESS METRICS
- Renewal rate: Target 92% of contracts up for renewal
- Early renewal: Bonus - secure before competitive pressure
- Churn: Understand reasons for lost accounts
- Reference: fleet_contract_pannonia.txt - volume below commitment, fuel quality complaint - retention actions in progress
