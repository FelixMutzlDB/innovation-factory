EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
ASM Cockpit Service Station Network
Document Version: 2.0 | All Staff Must Be Trained

OVERVIEW
Service stations present unique hazards: fuel, electricity, machinery, and public access. This document outlines response procedures for critical emergencies. All staff must complete annual emergency procedure training.

FIRE EMERGENCY

Fuel Fire (Pump, Dispenser, Forecourt)
1. Activate fire alarm if available
2. Call emergency services: 112 (EU) - state "fuel fire at service station"
3. Evacuate customers and staff to safe distance (minimum 50 meters)
4. Do not attempt to extinguish fuel fire with water
5. If trained and safe: Use foam or dry chemical extinguisher (Class B) - only if fire is small and contained
6. Shut off fuel supply if safe to access (emergency shut-off typically at tank or forecourt)
7. Do not re-enter until emergency services advise
8. Document: Time, location, cause if known, injuries

Building Fire (Shop, Office)
1. Activate alarm, call 112
2. Evacuate all persons - use nearest exit
3. Do not use elevators
4. Assemble at designated meeting point
5. Account for all staff and known customers
6. Do not re-enter until cleared

GAS LEAK (LPG or Natural Gas)
1. Do not operate electrical switches - risk of ignition
2. Evacuate immediately - upwind direction
3. Call 112 and gas supplier emergency number
4. Prevent ignition sources: No vehicles starting, no smoking
5. Cordon off area - prevent entry
6. Ventilation: Do not attempt to open doors/windows if gas present - leave to professionals
7. Document: Location of suspected leak, odor description, time

MEDICAL EMERGENCY

Customer or Staff Injury
1. Assess scene - ensure safe to approach
2. Call 112 for serious injury, bleeding, unconsciousness, chest pain, difficulty breathing
3. Do not move injured person unless in immediate danger
4. First aid: Apply only if trained - bleeding control, CPR if qualified
5. AED: If available and person unresponsive - follow AED instructions
6. Comfort: Stay with person until help arrives
7. Document: Name, injury, actions taken, witnesses

ROBBERY
1. Comply with perpetrator - no resistance
2. Activate silent alarm if available
3. Observe: Description, vehicle, direction of travel
4. Do not pursue
5. Call police after perpetrator leaves
6. Preserve scene - do not touch evidence
7. Support staff - offer counseling per policy
8. Document: Full report for police and ASM security

SEVERE WEATHER
- Lightning: Evacuate forecourt; customers may shelter in shop
- Flood: Shut off fuel supply if water approaching tanks; evacuate
- High wind: Secure loose items; consider forecourt closure if debris risk
- Document: Per incident reporting procedure

POST-INCIDENT
- Debrief staff within 48 hours
- Incident report in ASM Cockpit
- Review procedures if gaps identified
- Support: EAP available for staff after traumatic incident
