CASH HANDLING PROCEDURES
ASM Cockpit Service Station Network
Document Version: 2.2 | Security Classification: Confidential

OVERVIEW
Proper cash management protects assets and ensures reconciliation. Cash handling vulnerabilities are a primary loss source at retail fuel sites. These procedures apply to all staff handling cash.

CASH DRAWER PROCEDURES

Opening Float
- Standard float: 200 EUR (50 EUR in change, 150 EUR in notes)
- Count and verify at shift start
- Document in till log
- Report discrepancies to manager

During Shift
- Large notes (100 EUR, 200 EUR): Drop to safe when drawer exceeds 500 EUR
- Never leave till unattended with drawer open
- Cash in drawer: Maximum 800 EUR at any time
- Customer view: Position till to minimize visibility of cash

Closing and Reconciliation
- Count cash in presence of second person when possible
- Reconcile to POS report - variance must be explained
- Variance over 20 EUR: Document and report to manager
- Drop all cash to safe at shift end except opening float for next shift

SAFE PROCEDURES

Safe Access
- Combination known only to authorized staff (per station roster)
- Change combination when staff with access leaves
- Never share combination
- Safe room: Lock when unoccupied

Cash Drops
- Frequency: Per station risk assessment (typically every 2-4 hours or when drawer exceeds 500 EUR)
- Drop envelope: Seal, date, initial
- Log drop in ASM Cockpit or station log
- Do not transport large amounts alone - two-person rule for bank deposits

Safe Count
- Daily: Manager or designated staff
- Document count, compare to expected
- Investigate variances immediately
- Safe should reconcile to: Opening balance + drops - withdrawals

BANK DEPOSITS
- Schedule: Per station (typically 2-3 times weekly)
- Preparation: Count and verify, prepare deposit slip
- Transport: Two persons, varied route and time
- Armored car: For high-cash stations per contract
- Document: Deposit slip copy retained, logged in system

RECONCILIATION
- Daily: Till reconciliation, safe count
- Weekly: Full cash audit by manager
- Discrepancy: Investigate within 48 hours
- Theft suspicion: Report to regional security; do not accuse staff without evidence

SECURITY MEASURES
- CCTV coverage of till and safe area
- No personal bags in cash handling area
- Staff screening per policy
- Surprise cash counts by manager

RED FLAGS - REPORT IMMEDIATELY
- Unexplained repeated variances
- Refusal to take leave (potential cover-up)
- Lifestyle inconsistent with salary
- Manipulation of refunds or voids
