INCIDENT REPORT: CUSTOMER FUEL QUALITY COMPLAINT INVESTIGATION
Station: HU-BP-001 (referenced); Complaint originated from fleet customer
Date: 28 January 2025
Incident ID: INC-2025-00201
Classification: Product Quality / Customer Relations

SUMMARY
Fleet customer Pannonia Freight reported fuel quality concerns following a delivery to 12 vehicles at station HU-BP-001 on 26 January 2025. Customer reported rough engine performance and check engine lights on 3 vehicles. Investigation was initiated per standard procedure.

COMPLAINT DETAILS
- Reporter: Pannonia Freight fleet manager
- Vehicles affected: 3 of 12 fueled
- Product: Diesel
- Approximate volume: 180L total across 3 vehicles
- Symptoms: Rough idle, reduced power, check engine light
- Vehicle types: 2x MAN TGX, 1x Scania R450

INVESTIGATION PROCEDURE

Step 1: Tank and Product Verification (27 January)
- Tank 2 (Diesel) at HU-BP-001: Sample drawn from tank sump
- Visual inspection: Clear, no water separation, no sediment
- Lab analysis ordered: Water content, particulate, lubricity, cetane number
- Result: All parameters within specification

Step 2: Delivery History Review
- Last delivery to Tank 2: 24 January 2025, 18,000L from approved supplier
- Delivery acceptance: All checks passed, documentation complete
- No delivery between 24 January and 26 January

Step 3: Dispenser Verification
- Pumps 2, 4, 6 (Diesel) inspected
- Filters: Replaced 15 January (per schedule)
- No water in filter bowls
- Sample from dispenser: Within specification

Step 4: Cross-Reference
- Other customers fueled same pumps 26 January: No complaints
- Station HR-C-002 (same fuel supply chain): No complaints
- Supplier batch tracking: Batch from same refinery load delivered to 8 stations - no other reports

FINDINGS
- Station fuel quality: Within specification
- No evidence of contamination at HU-BP-001
- Possible causes for customer vehicles: Pre-existing condition, fuel from other source, cold weather fuel gelling (temperature was -5C on 26 January), or coincidental timing

RESOLUTION
- Customer informed of investigation results 30 January
- Offered: Fuel system flush for 3 vehicles at ASM expense (goodwill gesture)
- Customer accepted; vehicles serviced 5 February
- No recurrence reported
- Customer relationship maintained

DOCUMENTATION
- Full investigation report in ASM Cockpit
- Tank sample retained 90 days
- Supplier notified for their records
- Procedure updated: Add cold weather fuel advisory for fleet customers
