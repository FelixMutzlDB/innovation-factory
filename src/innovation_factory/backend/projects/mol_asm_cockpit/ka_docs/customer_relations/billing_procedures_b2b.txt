B2B INVOICING AND BILLING PROCEDURES
ASM Cockpit Customer Relations
Document Version: 2.1 | Last Updated: January 2025

OVERVIEW
Accurate and timely B2B invoicing is critical for fleet customer satisfaction. Billing errors are a leading cause of disputes and can damage relationships. This document covers invoicing cycles, payment terms, and credit management for fleet accounts.

INVOICING CYCLES

Weekly Invoicing
- Accounts: TransEuropa, Central Taxi, other high-transaction fleets
- Cut-off: Sunday 23:59
- Invoice generation: Monday morning
- Delivery: Email by Monday 12:00
- Detail: Transaction-level or summary per customer preference

Bi-Weekly Invoicing
- Accounts: Pannonia, mid-size fleets
- Cut-off: Alternating Sundays
- Invoice generation: Monday following cut-off
- Delivery: Within 24 hours of generation

Monthly Invoicing
- Accounts: Starline, Danubia, large consolidated accounts
- Cut-off: Last day of month
- Invoice generation: Within 5 business days
- Delivery: Email; portal access for some accounts

INVOICE CONTENT
- Header: Customer name, address, tax ID, contract reference
- Period: Invoice date range
- Summary: Total volume, total amount by product type
- Detail: Per station, per vehicle, or per transaction as per contract
- Taxes: Clearly stated; VAT breakdown
- Payment: Due date, bank details, reference for payment
- Contact: Billing inquiry contact

PAYMENT TERMS
- Standard: 14-21 days from invoice date
- Extended: 30 days for premium accounts (e.g., Starline)
- Method: Bank transfer standard; no cash, no card for B2B
- Currency: Per contract - HUF, EUR, or multi-currency
- Reference: Customer must include invoice number on payment

CREDIT MANAGEMENT

Credit Limits
- New accounts: Credit check before contract
- Limit: Based on expected monthly volume, payment terms
- Review: Annual or when volume increases significantly
- Hold: New accounts may require deposit or shorter terms initially

Late Payment
- Day 1-7 overdue: Reminder email
- Day 8-14: Phone call from account manager
- Day 15-21: Escalation to credit team; possible service restriction
- Day 22+: Formal demand; potential suspension of fueling privileges
- Interest: Late payment interest per contract (typically 8-10% annual)

Dispute Resolution
- Customer disputes invoice: Document in writing
- Investigation: Within 5 business days
- Credit note: If error confirmed; process within 3 business days
- Reference: customer_complaint_taxi_invoicing.txt for dispute process

RECONCILIATION
- Payment allocation: Match payment to invoices
- Short payment: Contact customer; may be partial payment or error
- Overpayment: Credit to account or refund per customer preference
- Unallocated: Investigate within 5 days

REPORTING
- Aging report: Weekly for credit team
- DSO: Days sales outstanding by account
- Bad debt: Write-off process per finance policy
- Dashboard: ASM Cockpit B2B billing module

SPECIAL REQUIREMENTS
- 24/7 invoicing: Central Taxi and similar - invoice available for all shifts
- Multi-country: Consolidated invoice with country breakdown
- Vehicle-level: Required for some fleets (driver cost allocation)
- Reference: fleet_contract_central_taxi.txt for 24/7 requirement
