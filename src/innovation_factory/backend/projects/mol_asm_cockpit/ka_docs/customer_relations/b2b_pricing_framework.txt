B2B FLEET PRICING FRAMEWORK
ASM Cockpit Customer Relations
Document Version: 2.0 | Confidential - Internal Use Only

OVERVIEW
This document outlines the pricing framework for B2B fleet customers. Pricing is negotiated within these guidelines. Deviations require regional commercial approval. Framework ensures consistency while allowing flexibility for strategic accounts.

VOLUME DISCOUNT TIERS

Base Tiers (Monthly Diesel Equivalent)
- Bronze: 10,000 - 24,999 liters - 3% discount
- Silver: 25,000 - 49,999 liters - 4.5% discount
- Gold: 50,000 - 99,999 liters - 6% discount
- Platinum: 100,000 - 199,999 liters - 8% discount
- Premium: 200,000+ liters - 9-10% discount (negotiated)

Adjustments
- Gasoline/LPG mix: Slightly lower discount (margin structure differs)
- Multi-country: +0.5% for cross-border complexity
- Commitment length: +0.25% for 3-year vs. 1-year commitment
- Payment terms: Faster payment (7-14 days) may support +0.25%

NEGOTIATION GUIDELINES

Authority Levels
- Account manager: Up to published tier
- Regional commercial: +0.5% above tier, non-standard terms
- Head of B2B: +1% above tier, strategic deals
- Board: Above 10% discount, exceptional circumstances

Competitive Response
- When customer presents competitor quote: Verify quote authenticity
- Match only when: Volume confirmed, contract term acceptable
- Do not lead with discount: Establish value first
- Walk-away: Know minimum acceptable terms per account

Value Beyond Price
- Payment terms: Extended terms have cost - factor in
- Volume commitment: Higher commitment justifies higher discount
- Station network: Access to high-demand locations
- Service: Account management, reporting, support
- Loyalty: Points value for fleet

PRICING COMPONENTS
- Base: Pump price (excluding taxes) at station
- Discount: Applied to base
- Net price: Base minus discount
- Taxes: Applied to net; customer responsibility
- Cross-border: Additional fee unless waived (platinum/premium)

CONTRACT TERMS
- Minimum term: 12 months standard; 24-36 for higher discounts
- Volume commitment: Typically 80-90% of stated volume
- Shortfall: Discount review or contract termination
- Price mechanism: Pump price minus discount; or fixed margin (rare)
- Review: Annual price review clause standard

DOCUMENTATION
- All negotiated terms in contract
- Discount approval documented in ASM Cockpit
- Audit trail for compliance
